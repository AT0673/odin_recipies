Sloppy Joes

Description

A Sloppy Joe is a sandwich consisting of ground beef and onions in a tomato-based sauce served on a hamburger bun. The sandwich likely originated in Sioux City, Iowa in the 1930s. According to legend, the "loose meat sandwich" was the creation of a cook named Joe.

Ingredients

  • Beef
  • Onion
  • Buns
  • Ketchup and Mustard
  • Salt and Pepper
  • Bell Pepper
How to Prepare
  1. In a large skillet, cook 1 pound of ground beef over medium heat until browned. Drain any excess fat.
  2. Add 1 chopped onion and 1/2 chopped green bell pepper to the skillet. Cook until the vegetables are tender, about 5 minutes.
  3. Stir in 1 cup of ketchup, 1 tablespoon of Worcestershire sauce, 1 tablespoon of brown sugar, 1 teaspoon of mustard, and 1/2 teaspoon of garlic powder. Mix well and bring to a simmer.
  4. Reduce the heat to low and let the mixture simmer for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens to your desired consistency.
  5. Spoon the mixture onto hamburger buns and serve hot. Enjoy your homemade Sloppy Joes!
